Impact of Discounts on Sales, Profitability, and Customer Behavior

This project analyzes an e-commerce store's sales data to determine the most profitable product category, the impact of discounts on sales and profitability, customer purchase trends, and the influence of social media and shipping preferences on purchasing decisions.

Tools Used:

  • SQL: Data extraction, transformation, and analysis.
  • Excel: Data cleaning and preprocessing.
  • Tableau: Data visualization and insights presentation.

Dataset Details:

Dataset Name: ecommerce_consumer

Columns:

  • Customer_ID, Age, Gender, Income_Level, Marital_Status
  • Purchase_Category, Purchase_Amount, Discount_Used (TRUE/FALSE)
  • Frequency_of_Purchase, Brand_Loyalty, Product_Rating
  • Social_Media_Influence, Return_Rate, Customer_Satisfaction
  • Engagement_with_Ads, Device_Used_for_Shopping, Payment_Method
  • Date, Customer_Loyalty_Program_Member, Purchase_Intent

Data Cleaning Steps:

  • Removed duplicate entries.
  • Handled missing values using category-wise averages.
  • Converted date format to YYYY-MM-DD.
  • Standardized categorical data for consistency.

SQL Queries for Analysis:

Finding the Most Profitable Product Category

This query calculates the total profit for each product category and identifies the most profitable one.

SELECT purchase_category,
       SUM(purchase_amount - cost_price) AS total_profit
FROM ecommerce_consumer
GROUP BY purchase_category
ORDER BY total_profit DESC
LIMIT 1;

Impact of Discounts on Sales

This query compares total sales with and without discounts to measure the effectiveness of discount campaigns.

SELECT purchase_category,
       SUM(CASE WHEN discount_used = TRUE THEN purchase_amount ELSE 0 END) AS total_sales_with_discount,
       SUM(CASE WHEN discount_used = FALSE THEN purchase_amount ELSE 0 END) AS total_sales_without_discount
FROM ecommerce_consumer
GROUP BY purchase_category
ORDER BY total_sales_without_discount DESC;

Comparing Average Purchase Amount With and Without Discounts

This query calculates the average purchase amount for transactions with and without discounts.

SELECT discount_used, AVG(purchase_amount) AS avg_purchase_amount, COUNT(*) AS num_transactions
FROM ecommerce_consumer
GROUP BY discount_used;

Customer Segmentation Based on Purchase Behavior

This query segments customers based on income level and calculates the average spending per customer group.

SELECT income_level, COUNT(DISTINCT customer_id) AS num_customers,
       AVG(purchase_amount) AS avg_purchase_per_customer
FROM ecommerce_consumer
GROUP BY income_level
ORDER BY avg_purchase_per_customer DESC;

Key Insights & Conclusion:

  • Most profitable product category: Electronics had the highest revenue and net profit.
  • Impact of discounts: Discounts increased transactions but reduced profit margins.
  • Customer behavior trends: High-income customers spend more per purchase, while middle-income customers buy more frequently with discounts.
  • Effect of social media: Customers engaged with ads spend more.
  • Shipping preferences: Faster shipping results in quicker purchase decisions.
