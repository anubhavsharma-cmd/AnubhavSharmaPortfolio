Impact of Discounts on Sales, Profitability, and Customer Behavior

This project aims to analyze sales data from an e-commerce store to understand the impact of discounts on sales and profitability, identify the most profitable product categories, and explore customer purchase behavior.

The dataset contains customer demographics, purchase details, discount usage, engagement with ads, shipping preferences, and more. By leveraging SQL, Excel, and Tableau, we extract insights that help optimize pricing strategies, marketing efforts, and customer retention approaches.

🔹 Tools Used

  • SQL: Extracting and analyzing e-commerce sales data.
  • Excel: Data cleaning, handling missing values, and preprocessing.
  • Tableau: Visualization of key findings and trends.

📌 Dataset Overview

The dataset includes multiple aspects of customer transactions:

  • Customer Details: Age, Gender, Income Level, Marital Status.
  • Purchase Information: Product Category, Purchase Amount, Discount Used.
  • Customer Engagement: Time Spent on Research, Social Media Influence, Ad Engagement.
  • Shipping & Payment: Preferred Shipping Method, Payment Type, Return Rate.

🛠 Data Cleaning & Preparation

To ensure accurate analysis, we performed the following steps:

  • Removed duplicate customer records and invalid transactions.
  • Handled missing values using median imputation for numerical columns.
  • Converted all date values to a standard YYYY-MM-DD format.
  • Normalized categorical variables such as "High", "Medium", "Low" for income levels.

🔎 SQL Queries for Analysis

📊 Finding the Most Profitable Product Category

This query calculates total profit for each product category by subtracting cost price from purchase amounts. We then sort the results in descending order to identify the most profitable category. Businesses can use this insight to focus marketing efforts on high-profit products. 

SELECT purchase_category,
       SUM(purchase_amount - cost_price) AS total_profit
FROM ecommerce_consumer
GROUP BY purchase_category
ORDER BY total_profit DESC
LIMIT 1;

📉 Impact of Discounts on Sales

This query compares total sales for products with and without discounts. By segmenting sales based on discount usage, we can assess whether discounts drive higher revenue. This helps in making data-driven decisions on pricing strategies. 

SELECT purchase_category,
       SUM(CASE WHEN discount_used = TRUE THEN purchase_amount ELSE 0 END) AS total_sales_with_discount,
       SUM(CASE WHEN discount_used = FALSE THEN purchase_amount ELSE 0 END) AS total_sales_without_discount
FROM ecommerce_consumer
GROUP BY purchase_category
ORDER BY total_sales_without_discount DESC;

📊 Key Insights & Conclusion

  • Most profitable category: The Electronics category generated the highest revenue and profit margins.
  • Discounts impact: While discounts increased the number of transactions, they reduced overall profit margins.
  • Customer behavior: High-income customers make larger purchases, whereas middle-income customers buy more frequently with discounts.
  • Marketing strategies: Customers influenced by social media and ad campaigns tend to have higher engagement rates and spending levels.
  • Shipping preferences: Customers who choose faster shipping options make quicker purchasing decisions.
